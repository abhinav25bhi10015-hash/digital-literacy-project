Case Study: UPI Payment Fraud in India
UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a common cybercrime in India where 
attackers trick victims into authorizing transactions that drain their bank accounts.
Typically, the crime begins with "social engineering." An attacker may pose as a buyer 
on a platform like OLX or a customer service agent.

The process usually follows these steps:
The Hook: The fraudster sends a "Collect Request" or a QR code to the victim, 
claiming it is for "receiving" a payment.
The Deception: They instruct the victim to scan the code or 
click the request and enter their UPI PIN.

The Theft: In reality, entering a PIN is only required for sending money, 
not receiving it. Once the PIN is entered, the funds are immediately transferred 
from the victim's account to the attacker's.

College students are often targeted because of their frequent use of 
digital payment apps for daily expenses. The consequences include immediate 
financial loss and the potential compromise of linked bank account details.
